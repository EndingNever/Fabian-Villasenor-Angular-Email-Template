Lancia Stratos HF

The Lancia Stratos is an Italian-made rally car, winning the World Rally Championship in 1974, 1975, and 1976. The HF stands for High Fidelity. The Stratos was the first car designed specifically for a rally competition, with a dedicated team composed of engineer Mike Parkes, rally driver Sandro Munari, and Nicola Materazzi, an engineer who would perform calculations on chassis, steering, suspension, and engine components.

The Stratos was powered by the Ferrari Dino V6 engine, producing between 275 hp for the original 12 valve version and 320 hp for the 24 valve version. Lancia produced 492 cars in order to be allowed to race in Group 4 (sports cars), which were sold to the public as The Lancia Stratos HF Stradale. Powered by the same Dino V6, the Stradale came with a lower state of tune, producing only about 190 hp, a 0-62 mph time of 6.8 seconds, and a top speed of 144 mph.
